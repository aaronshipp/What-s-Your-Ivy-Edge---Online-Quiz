The Observer

Cool Blue

Analytical, precise, and deeply thoughtful. You rely on facts and take pride in getting things exactly right.

If Cool Blue is your color, you're the person who actually reads the fine print — and probably highlights it, too. You think before you speak, lean on facts over feelings, and take genuine pride in getting things right. You rely on data over gut feelings, and accuracy matters to you — in your work, your words, and your decisions. Preparation is your superpower; you'd rather over-research than be caught off guard. You work best in calm, focused environments, and you tend to connect most easily with people who take things as seriously as you do.

The flip side? Owning a mistake can be tough. When you're wrong, your instinct is to go back and find more evidence rather than just move on. To the people who really know you, you are one of the most thoughtful and reliable people in the room. People who don't know you well, however, might read your quiet, measured style as cold or distant — but that's just you being thoughtful. Trust takes time for you, and that's completely okay.

Inner Drive
Doing things right, avoiding conflict with others
Goal
Correctness, predictability, the need to perceive and understand
Judges Others By
How people think
Influences Others By
Logical arguments, factual data
Value to a Team
Tests, clarifies, and evaluates
Over Uses
Analysis and research, what has worked before
Under Pressure
Worried, gets bogged down
Fear
Ridicule; abrupt change
More Effective With
Learning to decide without all the answers, greater understanding of people, enthusiasm
Opposite Style
Inspirer

Personal Thoughts

What I like about being an Observer

  • I have a strong sense of integrity and do what I think is right, even when others disagree
  • I'm not motivated by appearances or what others think I should want
  • I'm able to see situations clearly and without letting emotions cloud my judgment

What I don't like about being an Observer

  • I sometimes come across as defensive or cynical, even when I don't mean to
  • I struggle to say exactly what I mean in the moment
  • Social situations I didn't opt into can feel exhausting and uncomfortable
  • It bothers me when people who don't work as carefully as I do still get ahead

Things I would never do

  • Throw a surprise party or take a job in sales
  • Volunteer to be the host at a big social event
  • Spend my free time keeping up with celebrity drama

How to relate with me

  • Give me time alone to process my thoughts and feelings
  • Don't mistake my quietness for rudeness — I just move at my own pace
  • Be direct — I respect straightforwardness more than almost anything
  • When I seem frustrated, I'm almost always frustrated with myself

Things people say about me

  • "They're a loner — but totally content spending hours deep in something that interests them."
  • "Honestly, they're one of the most knowledgeable people I know."
  • "They're quietly perceptive — they see things the rest of us walk right past."

Things to affirm to yourself

  • I'll approach others with openness instead of assumptions.
  • Letting people in actually helps me grow.
  • I don't have to win every intellectual exchange.

The Reformer

Royal Purple

Driven by excellence. You set the bar high, think fast, and can't understand why others wouldn't do the same.

Royal Purple Reformers are wired for excellence — and not the participation-trophy kind. You set the bar high for yourself and genuinely can't understand why others wouldn't do the same. Your mind moves fast, jumps between ideas, and almost always lands on a creative solution that no one else considered. You need space to think and the freedom to question — being micromanaged is basically your kryptonite.

The tension for Reformers is that the same drive that makes you exceptional can make you hard to be around. You can become blunt when frustrated, dismissive when your ideas are ignored, and slow to let go of a position even when the evidence says otherwise. Your standards, which are genuinely your greatest asset, can also make you hard on yourself and impatient with people who operate differently. Others might see you as intense or overbearing — but underneath that is someone who genuinely cares about doing things the right way.

Self-criticism and the need to be in charge

Overly critical, impatient, and negative
Fear
Disorganization and failure
More Effective With
Patience and understanding of how others like to work
Opposite Style
Helper

Personal Thoughts

What I like about being a Reformer

  • I'm self-disciplined and have a track record of actually getting things done
  • I'm always pushing myself to be the best version of who I can be
  • I'm reliable, responsible, and fully committed to what I take on
  • I don't compromise on ethics. I know what's right and I do it

What I don't like about being a Reformer

  • I get genuinely frustrated when others don't match my effort level
  • I can get so intense about everything that I forget to breathe
  • I hold high expectations for myself and others, and the disappointment when they aren't met is real
  • I'm a perfectionist, and that's not always easy to live with or work alongside

Things I would never do

  • Stop caring about my own interests
  • Show up late
  • Pretend criticism doesn't sting
  • Forgive myself quickly for a mistake

How to relate with me

  • Remind me that it's okay to take things a little less seriously sometimes
  • Recognize my effort, not just the outcome
  • Step in and help carry the load without me having to ask
  • Help me get out of my own head when I'm being too critical

Things people say about me

  • "You can hand them an assignment and walk away. It'll be done, and done well."
  • "They won't do something they believe is wrong. Period."
  • "They set the standard. Everyone else is just trying to keep up."

Things to affirm to yourself

  • It's okay to laugh at myself sometimes.
  • Taking myself less seriously doesn't make me less serious about what matters.
  • I can make mistakes and still be someone worth looking up to.

The Director

Fiery Red

Built for action. Decisive, driven, and completely comfortable leading from the front.

Fiery Red Directors are built for action. You are decisive, driven, and completely comfortable leading from the front. Routine bores you. Rules that don't make sense frustrate you. You want authority, challenge, and the freedom to make things happen your way. When a problem needs solving, you cut straight to the point and push hard until it's done.

The thing about Directors is that your intensity, which is one of your greatest strengths, can feel like "a lot" to people who operate differently. You can come across as impatient, demanding, or even intimidating — especially when your standards aren't being met; others may feel bulldozed. Learning to slow down, listen, and make room for others' approaches isn't natural for you, but it's where your real growth lives. Your energy is genuinely powerful — the work is learning to channel it in ways that bring people with you rather than push them away.

Inner Drive
Take charge of the world around them and compete to win
Goal
Being in control, independence, and pushing for change
Judges Others By
Their ability to get the task done
Influences Others By
Force of character, persistence, and drive
Value to a Team
Bold attitude, imagination, and the drive to make things happen
Over Uses
Impatience, going it alone, and pushing too hard
Under Pressure
Aggressive, stubborn, and quick to push back
Fear
Losing control. Being seen as soft or not serious
More Effective With
Patience, genuine concern for others, and humility about the fact that they don't always have the right answer
Opposite Style
Supporter

Personal Thoughts

What I like about being a Director

  • When something needs to happen, I make it happen
  • The people close to me know I'll always have their back
  • I know who I am and I don't need anyone's approval to feel that way

What I don't like about being a Director

  • My directness sometimes lands harder than I intend, and people pull away
  • Sitting with someone else's opinion long enough to really hear it is hard for me
  • Incompetence and slow movement make me impatient in ways I know aren't always fair

Things I would never do

  • Genuinely mean it when I say 'let's do it your way'
  • Keep my opinion to myself when I know something is wrong
  • Play to tie

How to relate with me

  • Hold your ground with me — I respect confidence, not compliance
  • Know that I have a softer side, even if I don't lead with it
  • Loyalty matters more to me than almost anything
  • My bluntness is never personal — it's just efficiency

Things people say about me

  • "I can say exactly what I'm thinking around them. They actually want that."
  • "They lead clearly and fairly. You always know where you stand."
  • "They'll say the uncomfortable thing that everyone else is thinking but won't say."

Things to affirm to yourself

  • Good relationships are worth bending for.
  • The first idea isn't always the best one — including mine.
  • Letting people see my softer side doesn't make me less strong.

The Motivator

Radiant Orange

The energy in the room. You inspire, rally, and make even reluctant crowds start to believe.

Radiant Orange Motivators are the energy in the room. You're at your best when you're around people — inspiring them, rallying them, getting them excited about what's possible. You're a natural communicator who can read a group, adapt on the fly, and make even a reluctant crowd start to believe. You don't just want to succeed; you want to succeed visibly, with a team behind you who feels good about what you've built together. Results matter to you, but so do the people you're working with — and that combination is rare.

What Motivators have to watch is the tendency to be too optimistic — about what you can deliver, about how others will perform, about how long things will take. Your confidence is genuinely infectious, but it can occasionally outrun reality. You can also get bored quickly when the novelty wears off. The antidote is structure: a little more planning, a little more patience, and a little more attention to the details you'd rather skip. Slowing down to build structure isn't your favorite thing — but it's what turns your energy into lasting results.

Inner Drive
To express an enthusiastic and charismatic presence
Goal
Recognition and keeping an eye on the big picture
Judges Others By
Their own idea of what success looks like
Influences Others By
Enthusiasm for what's possible; recognition and praise
Value to a Team
Influences and inspires others to achieve their goals
Over Uses
Assertiveness and big-picture thinking at the expense of details
Under Pressure
Bored easily, impatient, and aggressive
Fear
Not being given real responsibility, and failure
More Effective With
Structure and follow-through, understanding how processes work, and slowing down enough to put things in writing
Opposite Style
Coordinator

Personal Thoughts

What I like about being a Motivator

  • I'm genuinely optimistic — problems don't knock me out for long
  • I go after the things that scare me a little. That's how I know they're worth it
  • I have a wide range of passions and I'm not afraid to pursue all of them
  • I lead with generosity

What I don't like about being a Motivator

  • Inefficiency drives me crazy. I have very little patience for it
  • I sometimes perform a version of myself to make a good impression
  • There's never enough time to do everything I want to do
  • One-on-one intensity can feel confining to me after a while

Things I would never do

  • Let a meeting run inefficiently when I could step in
  • Spend a week in complete silence
  • Finish every project before starting the next one
  • Plan my week in a spreadsheet and actually follow it

How to relate with me

  • Give me room to breathe — freedom and friendship go a long way with me
  • Keep me engaged with interesting conversation
  • Let me be a little extra sometimes — it's how I connect
  • Challenge me intellectually and make me laugh
  • Don't try to change me. Get to know me

Things people say about me

  • "They make everyone feel like they matter."
  • "When they're not doing something adventurous, they're helping someone else with something."
  • "They're the person you call when you need to remember why life is good."

Things to affirm to yourself

  • I'll build in actual time to slow down.
  • I'll bring more balance into my life — not less enthusiasm, just more grounding.
  • Depth matters as much as breadth.

The Inspirer

Sunshine Gold

Outgoing, enthusiastic, and genuinely magnetic. You make rooms feel different when you walk in.

Sunshine Gold Inspirers are the people who make rooms feel different when they walk in. You are outgoing, enthusiastic, and genuinely magnetic — people are drawn to your energy without always being able to explain why. You build friendships fast, light up social spaces, and have a gift for making others feel seen and excited. You have a wide circle of acquaintances, and your ability to network and connect is something others genuinely admire and often can't quite replicate. You don't just promote your ideas; you make people want to believe in them too.

The challenge for Inspirers is that your energy can scatter. You leap into things with excitement and sometimes skip the step where you gather all the information first. You may start more things than you finish, take on more than you can realistically manage, and struggle to stay consistent when the initial excitement fades. Time and follow-through are the skills that will turn your natural magnetism into something lasting.

Wanting to connect with and be accepted by others
Goal
Popularity and approval
Judges Others By
How well they communicate and how much they care about people
Influences Others By
Praise and favors
Value to a Team
Lightens the mood; lifts others up and isn't afraid to advocate for themselves too
Over Uses
Optimism and flattery
Under Pressure
Careless, disorganized, and inconsistent
Fear
Feeling worthless or left out
More Effective With
Better time management, emotional self-regulation, and following through on what they say they'll do
Opposite Style

What I don't like about being an Inspirer

Things I would never do

How to relate with me

  • If you have to criticize me, do it gently
  • Find joy in the small stuff with me

Things people say about me

  • "They love the people in their life with everything they have."
  • "Funny, warm, and quietly sharp. They notice things."
  • "They love hard, and their people feel it. Their friendship feels like sunshine."

The Helper

Soothing Lime

You make teams better just by being in them. Sensitive, steady, and the kind of person who actually follows through.

If Soothing Lime is your color, you are someone who makes teams better just by being in them. You're sensitive to what others need, steady under most kinds of pressure, and the kind of person who actually follows through on 'let me know if you need anything.' You prefer collaborative environments and do your best work when you feel secure, supported, and appreciated. You work through relationships, not around them, and you have a rare ability to hold space for people who are struggling without making them feel judged.

What Helpers have to watch is the tendency to define themselves through service to others. It can be hard for you to make decisions without checking in with the group, to push back when you're being taken advantage of, or to set limits on how much you give. People sometimes mistake your steadiness for passivity — but you're not passive, you're just selective about when and where you expend your energy. Learning to advocate for your own needs — with the same generosity you show everyone else — is the work.

Understanding others and empathy
Goal
Close relationships, low-pressure environments, being of service to others
Judges Others By
Loyalty and how genuine they are
Influences Others By
Offering help and a listening ear
Value to a Team
Dependable, sincere, loyal
Over Uses
Being too tolerant and making everything personal
Under Pressure
Passive, resentful, and prone to feeling like a victim
Fear
Conflict, high pressure
More Effective With
Honest feedback, knowing when to step back, and staying focused on getting the job done
Opposite Style

Things people say about me

  • "Being around them feels easy. They just let you be who you are."
  • "They care about the things worth caring about."

Things to affirm to yourself

The Supporter

Earth Green

The steady one. You show up consistently, follow through reliably, and make everyone around you feel at ease.

Earth Green Supporters are the steady ones — the people who show up consistently, follow through reliably, and make everyone around them feel at ease. You are patient, considerate, and genuinely easy to get along with. You build your closest relationships slowly and intentionally, and the people lucky enough to be in your inner circle know you'll go the extra mile for them without being asked.

Where Supporters can struggle is with change and urgency. You prefer familiar environments, predictable routines, and the chance to move at your own pace. When things shift quickly or without warning, you can dig in and resist, even when change would genuinely benefit you — not out of stubbornness, but out of a genuine need to feel grounded before moving forward. Pushing yourself toward new experiences, even uncomfortable ones, is where your growth lives.

Influences Others By
Consistency and being easy to be around

Over Uses
Playing it safe and quietly resisting change
Under Pressure
Goes through the motions of adapting without really buying in
Fear
Change, chaos, and feeling unnecessary
More Effective With
Willingness to speak up and share ideas; self-confidence built through positive reinforcement
Opposite Style
Director

Personal Thoughts

What I like about being a Supporter

  • I'm responsible and I take my commitments seriously
  • I'm smart, loyal, and I have a sense of humor that surprises people
  • I can get along with most people and feel in harmony with the world

What I don't like about being a Supporter

  • Fear of getting it wrong can lead me to procrastinate more than I'd like
  • I worry about being overlooked or taken advantage of
  • My mind can spiral into worst-case scenarios faster than I'd like

Things I would never do

How to relate with me

  • Don't frame my carefulness as a flaw
  • Let me finish what I have to say, even if it doesn't make sense to you
  • Don't rush me into anything new. Ease me in

Things people say about me

  • "The most reliable, trustworthy, hardworking person I know."
  • "I never feel that they are judging me; they are very patient and perceptive."
  • "Nothing gets past them. They see it all, even if it seems like they don't."

Things to affirm to yourself

  • Taking risks is part of growing, and I can handle it.
  • Worry is not the same as preparation.
  • I know more than I give myself credit for.

The Coordinator

Pragmatic Teal

Loyal, disciplined, and thorough. You take your commitments seriously and don't make decisions lightly.

If Pragmatic Teal is your color, you are someone who takes your commitments seriously — to your work, your people, and your principles. You are loyal, disciplined, and thorough — someone with a strong personal value system who doesn't make decisions lightly. You are careful, systematic, and precise, and you hold yourself to high standards without needing everyone to know it. You work best in environments that value structure, give you time to think, and don't expect you to make snap decisions.

Where Coordinators can get stuck is in the worry zone. You're prone to overthinking decisions, second-guessing yourself after the fact, and catastrophizing outcomes that are unlikely to happen. Your caution is a genuine strength in the right context — but it can tip into anxiety when the environment is chaotic or the expectations are unclear. Learning to trust yourself, even without a guarantee, is the growth edge that changes everything. People who don't know you well may see you as reserved or overly cautious — but those who do recognize someone who is trustworthy, meticulous, and built for the long haul.

Inner Drive
Avoiding conflict and keeping things running smoothly
Goal
Correctness and orderliness
Judges Others By
How sharp and perceptive they are
Influences Others By
Facts, logical arguments, and a step-by-step approach
Value to a Team
Brings clarity, gathers the right information, and tests ideas before committing
Over Uses
Over-analyzing and needing everything to be perfectly organized
Under Pressure
Anxious, detail-obsessed, hard to reassure
Fear
Snap decisions and anything that feels irrational or reckless
More Effective With
Self-confidence, clear expectations, genuine appreciation, and a calm, structured environment
Opposite Style
Motivator
